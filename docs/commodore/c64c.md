# Commodore 64C (NTSC)

![Commodore 64C at power-on](images/c64c.jpg)

- **`make kernel MACHINE=c64c`** — Commodore Business Machines
- **Year**: 1986
- **Manufacturer**: Commodore Business Machines
- **Television**: NTSC

## At power-on

Commodore 64 BASIC V2, `READY.` — the IEC disk bus boots empty (`-iec8
""`), so no drive romset is required to reach BASIC. The 64C is the
cost-reduced restyle of the original NTSC `c64`; its ROM revision is the
same, so the sign-on banner and free-memory figure match the standard
machine. The visible difference is inside the case, not on the screen: the
64C merges BASIC and the KERNAL into a single 16 KB part.

## Required assets

- `roms/c64c.zip`

  | ROM | CRC32 |
  |---|---|
  | `251913-01.u4` (basic+kernal) | `0010ec31` |
  | `901225-01.u5` (chargen) | `ec4272ee` |
  | `252715-01.u8` (PLA) | `54c89351` |

  A distinct romset — not a `#define` alias of `rom_c64`. The 64C's
  defining part is the combined `251913-01.u4`, a single 16 KB ROM holding
  both BASIC and the KERNAL (the original `c64` keeps them in two 8 KB
  parts). The chargen is byte-identical to the NTSC `c64`; the PLA content
  is the standard C64 PLA (identical to `c64`'s `906114-01.u17`), which the
  driver expects here under the `252715-01.u8` filename. The kernal region
  has BIOS choices — the default `cbm` (Original) is baked; the alternate
  `pdc` (ProLogic-DOS Classic) is not shipped.
